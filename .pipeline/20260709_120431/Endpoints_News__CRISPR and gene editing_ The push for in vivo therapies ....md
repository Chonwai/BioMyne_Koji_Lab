# CRISPR and gene editing: The push for in vivo therapies

Gene editing has moved beyond the lab and into the clinic, but the field is still working through fundamental challenges of delivery, safety and scale. Vertex and CRISPR Therapeutics’ Casgevy proved the concept for _ex vivo_ editing in sickle cell disease, and now the race is on for _in vivo_ approaches that can edit genes directly inside the body. Startups from Jennifer Doudna’s Azalea to Patrick Hsu’s Stylus Medicine are pushing past traditional CRISPR with gene writing, epigenetic editing and novel delivery platforms. China is a growing force, with companies like YolTech and AccurEdit advancing their own editing programs. Endpoints tracks gene editing from foundational science to clinical milestones and the companies betting on the next generation of genetic medicines.
