# Race to cure HBV moves to targeting the reservoir

## Precision cuts HBV cccDNA while Tune and nChroma aim to shut it down with CRISPR‑based epigenetic silencers

By Danielle Golovin, Senior Biopharma Analyst

July 8, 2026 9:01 PM EDT

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

There’s now compelling evidence that eliminating viral transcripts with antisense oligonucleotides can lead to a functional HBV cure in some patients, and early clinical data from _in vivo_ gene editing and epigenetic silencing therapies suggest that directly targeting the viral DNA reservoir could take that progress a step further.

Bepirovirsen from GSK plc (LSE:GSK; NYSE:GSK) and Ionis Pharmaceuticals Inc. (NASDAQ:IONS), PBGENE‑HBV from Precision Biosciences Inc. (NASDAQ:DTIL), and epigenetic silencers from [Tune Therapeutics Inc.](https://profiles.biocentury.com/companies/tune_therapeutics_inc) and nChroma Bio Inc. have all converged on chronic HBV as a proving ground, positioning the disease as a key proof-of-concept indication for next-generation nucleic acid and genome editing platforms...
