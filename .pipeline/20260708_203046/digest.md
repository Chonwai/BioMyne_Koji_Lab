### GOP lawmakers push Trump's FDA to make sure clinical trials are diverse
**Priority:** medium | **Tags:** clinical trials, FDA policy
**URL:** https://www.statnews.com/2026/07/06/republican-lawmakers-attempt-clinical-trial-diversity-protection/

Republican members of Congress have introduced language in a new FDA funding bill requiring that clinical trials enroll participants representative of the patients who will use tested drugs and devices. The effort is designed to protect this principle from the Trump administration's broader crackdown on diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) initiatives—HHS began purging all DEI references from agency websites days after Trump took office. The underlying mandate traces to a Biden-era law that has not yet been implemented by the FDA. Supporters distinguish clinical trial enrollment requirements from politically charged DEI programs, arguing they simply ensure trial populations reflect real-world patients. Until Congress acts or HHS implements the rule, sponsors navigating clinical trial diversity face regulatory uncertainty under the new administration.

### STAT Plus: FDA approves Vera Therapeutics kidney disease treatment
**Priority:** high | **Tags:** drug development, chronic diseases, pharmaceuticals, biotechnology
**URL:** https://www.statnews.com/2026/07/07/vera-therapeutics-kidney-disease-treatment-approval/

The FDA has approved Trutakna, a new medicine from biotech company Vera Therapeutics, for the treatment of IgA nephropathy (IgAN), a chronic autoimmune condition characterized by the buildup of immune antibodies in the kidneys. IgAN leads to progressive loss of kidney function and can cause organ failure requiring dialysis. The approval represents a significant milestone for Vera Therapeutics as it enters a therapeutic area attracting wide interest from multiple biotech companies. CEO Marshall Fordyce told STAT the company is excited to bring Trutakna to patients, with the interview conducted ahead of the FDA decision.
