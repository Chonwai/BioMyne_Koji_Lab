### Allergan moves Botox into Phase 3, despite mid-stage failure
**Priority:** medium | **Tags:** clinical trials, CNS, neuroscience
**URL:** https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/allergan-botox-phase-3-depression/439923

Allergan announced that its Phase 2 trial of Botox (onabotulinumtoxin A) for major depressive disorder failed to show statistical significance at either the 30-unit or 50-unit dose on the MADRS scale. Despite the null result, Allergan plans to advance Botox into a Phase 3 program, betting that a larger late-stage trial with the lower dose will achieve clinical significance.

### AstraZeneca joins obesity biotech SixPeaks at basecamp with $80M for buyout option
**Priority:** high | **Tags:** obesity, weight loss, drug development, venture capital, pharma M&A
**URL:** https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/astrazeneca-climbs-front-line-80m-new-weight-loss-biotech-sixpeaks

AstraZeneca committed $80 million to SixPeaks Bio, a Switzerland-based obesity biotech raising $110M total ($30M Series A led by Versant Ventures plus AstraZeneca's deal), in exchange for an acquisition option once the company reaches its first IND filing. SixPeaks is developing an activin IIA/B receptor antibody designed to produce weight loss while preserving skeletal muscle mass -- a key weakness of current market leaders like Novo Nordisk's Wegovy and Eli Lilly's Zepbound -- with preclinical mouse data showing best-in-class monotherapy efficacy.

### FDA Rejects Novartis’ Serelaxin
**Priority:** high | **Tags:** Translational Medicine, Drug Discovery
**URL:** https://www.genengnews.com/news/fda-rejects-novartis-serelaxin

The FDA formally rejected Novartis' acute heart failure treatment serelaxin (RLX030/Reasanz), issuing a Complete Response Letter citing insufficient efficacy evidence. This marks the fourth regulatory setback for serelaxin this year, after an advisory committee unanimously voted 11-0 to recommend rejection over concerns about relying on a single clinical trial and dismissing the drug's primary endpoint as exploratory rather than clinically meaningful.

Benchling launched three products at SynBioBeta 2026: One-Click Ordering (integrating design-to-order workflow with Twist Bioscience, Adaptyv, and Ginkgo Bioworks to close the gap between computational design and physical synthesis), Benchling Biologics (an end-to-end antibody R&D platform automating entity configuration and validation at scale), and a Data Analysis skill for its AI platform powered by Nicholas Larus-Stone.
