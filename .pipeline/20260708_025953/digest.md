### Chiesi buys into Arbor gene editing drug for rare kidney disease
**Priority:** high | **Tags:** gene editing, rare disease, oncology/biotech, M&A
**URL:** https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/arbor-chiesi-gene-editing-primary-hyperoxaluria-partnership/801948

Chiesi Group acquired global rights to ABO-101, an experimental gene editing therapy from Arbor Biotechnologies for primary hyperoxaluria type 1 (PH1), a rare genetic kidney disorder. Under the deal, Chiesi pays up to $115 million in upfront and near-term payments with potential for up to $2 billion more in downstream milestone payments and royalties. ABO-101 is designed as a one-time gene editing treatment that could replace current PH1 therapies requiring frequent injections.

### AstraZeneca CEO defends 'fantastic' Alexion buyout in face of $753M hit from dropped drug
**Priority:** high | **Tags:** rare disease, drug development, M&A integration, pipeline management
**URL:** https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/astrazeneca-resets-big-bets-taking-1b-charges-ex-alexion-fusion-assets-suffer-setbacks

AstraZeneca is taking a $753 million impairment charge after abandoning vemircopan, an anti-C5 complement inhibitor from its $39 billion Alexion acquisition, due to lack of efficacy in lupus nephritis and impaired hepatic function indications. CEO Pascal Soriot defended the Alexion acquisition as 'fantastic' despite the broader pipeline reset that includes terminating additional programs from the Alexion and Fusion Pharmaceuticals portfolios.

### Biology Has Evolved. Regulation Hasn’t. Here’s the Fallout
**Priority:** medium | **Tags:** regulatory policy, synthetic biology, FDA regulation, biotech startups
**URL:** https://www.synbiobeta.com/read/biology-has-evolved-regulation-hasnt-heres-the-fallout

SynBioBeta interviews three biotech regulation experts about the fragmented patchwork of U.S. regulatory agencies overseeing synthetic biology products, primarily the FDA, EPA, and USDA-APHIS operating under the 1986 Coordinated Framework.
